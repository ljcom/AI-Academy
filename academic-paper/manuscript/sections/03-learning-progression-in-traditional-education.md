3 Learning Progression in Traditional Education
In formal education, learning is typically organized around structured progression. Students advance through levels of understanding by demonstrating mastery of key concepts and skills, a principle central to mastery learning theory (Bloom, 1984).
A simplified representation of this progression is:
concept introduction → practice → reflection → mastery → progression
In this model, learners do not advance merely by receiving explanations. Instead, they must demonstrate the ability to apply knowledge, explain concepts, and solve related problems. This process ensures that learning involves both cognitive engagement and conceptual understanding.
The structured nature of academic learning environments contrasts with the open-ended nature of conversational AI systems. While AI provides immediate access to knowledge, it does not inherently support progression through stages of mastery.
This gap suggests the need for a new learning framework that combines the flexibility of conversational AI with the structured progression found in traditional educational systems.

Conversational AI and the Shift in Learning Interaction
The emergence of conversational AI represents a fundamental shift in how learners interact with knowledge systems. Traditionally, digital learning environments have relied on static content formats such as textbooks, lecture slides, or recorded videos. These materials present information in a largely one-directional manner, where learners consume content and later demonstrate understanding through exercises or examinations.

Conversational AI introduces a different interaction paradigm. Instead of passively receiving information, learners can engage in dynamic dialogue with an AI system capable of responding to questions, generating explanations, and adapting responses to the learner’s level of understanding. This interaction creates the possibility of a more personalized learning experience.

However, while conversational interaction increases accessibility and flexibility, it does not automatically guarantee deeper learning. Without structured guidance, learners may move quickly between topics without developing a coherent conceptual framework. As a result, conversational AI must be complemented with mechanisms that support structured learning progression.
